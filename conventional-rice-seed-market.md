<p><p>The conventional rice seed market comprises three main types: Indica, Japonica, and Glutinous rice. Indica rice is long-grained, commonly cultivated in tropical regions, and is known for its high yield and aroma. Japonica rice has a short grain, preferred for its sticky texture and is predominantly grown in temperate climates. Glutinous rice, characterized by its high amylopectin content, is sticky when cooked and often used in Asian desserts and dishes. Each type caters to different culinary needs and regional preferences.</p></p>
