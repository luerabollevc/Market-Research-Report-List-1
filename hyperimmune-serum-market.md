<p><p>The hyperimmune serum market comprises various types of immunoglobulins tailored for specific infections and conditions. Hepatitis B immunoglobulins serve as passive immunity against hepatitis B virus. Rabies immunoglobulins provide critical protection post-exposure to rabies. Tetanus immunoglobulins help prevent tetanus infection following injury. Rho(D) immunoglobulins are administered to Rh-negative mothers to prevent complications in Rh-positive newborns. Other immunoglobulins may target additional viral or bacterial infections, contributing to the overall effectiveness of passive immunization treatment strategies.</p></p>
